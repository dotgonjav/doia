(01) — The studio

One studio. One interior architect. Full attention for your project.

In the Mongolian nomadic tradition, a home is warm, considered and in full harmony with its surroundings. Nothing more. Nothing less.

That idea has remained the measure for every interior created here. Honest, raw materials that smell, breathe and evolve — chosen with care for the way you live. On every project you work directly with one interior architect, from the first conversation to the final detail.

(02) — Who is it for?

An interior that is entirely yours.

You know what you don't want. A generic interior. Something that looks good in photos but never truly feels like home. A designer who presents three moodboards and asks you to choose.

Here it works differently. Your project begins with a real conversation, not a brief — and with attention for the things that don't appear on moodboards: how you move through your space in the morning, what makes you linger in a room, what you always wanted but never found the words for.

What follows is an interior built from honest materials, attuned to how you live — not to how it photographs.

(03) — Material

Material you feel before you see it.

Choosing a material is not an aesthetic decision. It shapes how a space feels every day — and what it still has to say ten, twenty years from now.

Natural materials don't age, they mature. Oak that lasts for generations, natural stone that never goes out of style, clay and linen that can be repaired rather than replaced — from renewable sources, without off-gassing finishes. Replacing less means wasting less; here, durability is not an addition, but the result of choosing well.

Smell

Untreated wood and natural stone carry a subtle, living scent — a room that smells of what it ís, not of varnish or glue.

Temperature

Stone holds its coolness in summer, wood carries warmth before you touch it. Material that moves with the season.

Texture

The grain of oak, the tooth of clay, the hand of linen — surfaces the fingertips recognise and that invite touch.

(04) — What drives the work

Quality over quantity. Always.

That same care takes time. That is why only a limited number of projects are given a place each year — not to create exclusivity, but because real attention cannot be scaled.

So your project receives the full weight it deserves.
